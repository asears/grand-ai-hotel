## Design Philosophy

These cards follow the Wes Anderson aesthetic established in The Grand Budapest Terminal project:

### Color Palette
- **Plum Purple (#8B4789)** - M. Gustave, sophistication
- **Deep Burgundy (#800020)** - Zero, dedication
- **Soft Pink (#FFB6C1)** - Agatha, delight
- **Dark Crimson (#990000)** - Dmitri, challenge
- **Military Green (#4B5320)** - Henckels, duty
- **Charcoal Grey (#36454F)** - Ludwig, precision
- **Antique Gold (#C5B358)** - Serge X., analysis
- **Cream (#F5E6D3)** - Background, elegance

### Art Deco Elements
- Geometric borders and corner ornaments
- Symmetrical compositions
- Gold and muted color accents
- Period-appropriate typography
- Radiating lines suggesting illumination or importance

### Technical Specifications
- **Format:** SVG (Scalable Vector Graphics)
- **Dimensions:** 300Ã—500 pixels (standard tarot card proportions)
- **Border:** Triple-line ornate border with art deco corners
- **Central composition:** Symmetrical design with symbolic elements
- **Typography:** Serif fonts for titles, monospace for code elements

## Creating New Cards

If you wish to create additional tarot cards for other tutorials, follow these guidelines:

### 1. Choose Your Concept
Select a concept that can be personified or symbolized (e.g., The Validator, The Deployer, The Tester)

### 2. Select Primary Colors
Choose 2-3 colors from the Wes Anderson palette that represent the card's theme

### 3. Design Elements
Include:
- Character or symbolic representation
- Tools or artifacts related to the concept
- Radiating lines or geometric patterns
- Art deco ornamental borders

### 4. SVG Structure
```xml
<svg width="300" height="500">
  <!-- Background -->
  <!-- Multiple ornate borders -->
  <!-- Art deco corners -->
  <!-- Card number (Roman numerals) -->
  <!-- Central symbolic image -->
  <!-- Title and subtitle -->
  <!-- Bottom ornament -->
</svg>
```
